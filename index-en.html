Scientific Projects




Tablets Tracker

Computational Methods For Predicting the Final Quality of A Pharmaceutical's Product During Production, Based On Its Sound And Elastic Properties.

Tablets Tracker

In this project, I and my research team, led by Prof. Dr. rer. nat. Ulrich Odefey and Prof. Dr. Miriam Pein-Hackelbusch, have successfully developed methods for monitoring the physical properties of a pharmaceutical product (texture, density, etc.). The core of the project is our algorithm, which picks up special sound waves propagating through the product. The algorithm, performs signal processing to extract features, which are then passed to our trained machine learning model that, based on these features, predicts the end-quality.

Bilirubin Scanner

Computer Vision Algorithm For Estimating Bilirubin Blood Levels From Digital Images.

Bilirubin Scanner

In this project, I have under the supervision of Prof. Dr. rer. nat. Helene Dörksen and the inIT Research Institute successfully developed an algorithm that performs image processing on digital images of human eyes under common lighting conditions to extract features, which are then passed on to a machine learning model trained by me, to approximate the blood bilirubin level of the person to whom the image belongs.

Sclera Detector

A Method For Segmenting The Sclera From Images Of Human Faces

Sclera Detector

In this project, I successfully developed an algorithm under the supervision of Prof. Dr. rer. nat. Helene Dörksen, which uses Deep Learning for eye detection as well as different image processing techniques to detect and isolate the pixels that correspond to the eye whites (i.e., the sclera) on any given image of a human face. This is an important step for various image analysis applications involving the sclera.
